# Claims

- Mechanism claim: humanoid contacts are scarce episode-level commitments across balance, manipulation, contact reliability, and recovery.
- Theory claim: local feasibility and fixed quotas are not sufficient statistics for deciding whether to spend or reserve a contact.
- Method claim: dual-price contact budgeting with recovery reserves, manipulation reserves, reliability, calibration, and fixed-budget screening improves robustness under contact scarcity.
- Evidence claim: the v5 local benchmark shows `0.06225` hard-success gain and `0.18505` hard-utility gain over the retained v4.1 baseline, with `10/10` paired hard-utility seed wins.
- Safety claim: budget violation, fall rate, reserve starvation, contact overuse, energy/contact cost, and calibration error decrease while balance margin and recovery success increase.
- Scope claim: strong local evidence, not final ICLR-main readiness.
